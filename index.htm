Instructions Part I

You are now in charge of managing a warehouse, which stocks a single type of perishable, life-saving medicine for emergency situations. At the beginning of every month, you decide how many doses of the life-saving medicine you want to order for the current month. The medicine doses are immediately delivered to your warehouse upon ordering, but expire within one month. Thus, every month you start out with an empty warehouse, because the expired medicine can no longer be given to patients. You cannot order more often than once a month.

In each month, there is an uncertain demand for the life-saving medicine you stock and this demand varies between 0 and 100 medicine doses. Demanded medicine doses are automatically taken out of your warehouse at the end of each month. If you have too many medicine doses, you will give away only what has been ordered (the demand) and the remaining medicine doses will expire and be removed from your inventory. If you have too little medicine doses in stock, i.e. the patients' needs are higher than your inventory; an emergency delivery is automatically initiated to cover the missing medicine doses. Demand is always met in full, no matter how much you have in stock. Buying and storing medicine doses in your warehouse has costs. Every medicine dose ordered and stored in the warehouse costs 10 points, while emergency deliveries are more expensive and cost 40 points per medicine dose. There is no refund for perished medicine.

You are in charge of managing the warehouse for a total of 40 months. Thus, for the 40 months you will take 40 decisions on how many medicine doses you want to order. You will start managing the warehouse with an initial budget of 100.000 points (the equivalent of 10€). All costs you incur for storing medicine doses in your warehouse or covering the cost of emergency deliveries will be deducted from this initial endowment. At the end of today’s study, your remaining points will be exchanged for Euros at a fixed rate of 10.000 points = 1€.

Example:
Assume that over the course of the 40 months, your decisions lead to total costs of 40.000 points. Starting out with an initial budget of 100.000 points leaves you with 60.000 points remaining after deducting total costs of all months. These 60.000 points are then exchanged at a rate of 10.0000 = 1€ for a final payoff for part I of 6€.

During the course of the 40 months, you always have access to the demand realizations as well as total costs of all previous rounds in the form of a history list as well as a corresponding graph.

In this task we ask you to imagine that you have been randomly paired with another person, whom we will refer to simply as the "Other." This other person is someone you do not know and that you will not knowingly meet in the future. Both you and the "Other" person will be making choices by choosing either the letter A, B, or C. Your own choices will produce points for both yourself and the "Other" person. Likewise, the other's choice will produce points for him/her and for you. Every point has value: the more points you receive, the better for you, and the more points the "Other" receives, the better for him/her.

Here's an example of how this task works:

A B C
You get 500 500 550
Other gets 100 500 300

In this example, if you choose A you would receive 500 points and the other would receive 100 points; if you chose B, you would receive 500 points and the other 500; and if you chose C, you would receive 550 points and the other 300. So, you see that your choice influences both the number of points you receive and the number of points the other receives. Before you begin making choices, please keep in mind that there are no right or wrong answers -- choose the option that you, for whatever reason, prefer most. Also, remember that the points have value: the more of them you accumulate the better for you. Likewise, from the "other's" point of view, the more points s/he accumulates, the better for him/her.
